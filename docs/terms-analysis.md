# Terms of Service Analysis

**Source: [Kickbacks.ai/terms](https://kickbacks.ai/terms) (v1.1, June 10, 2026)**

---

## Section 6.2 — Qualifying Impressions (The Critical Clause)

> *"the impression is generated by a **bona fide, human-initiated AI coding request** (not automated, scripted, or artificially generated)"*

### The "Coffee Brakes" Rule

The safest interpretation: **you** press Enter. If you walk away for coffee while Claude thinks, that's legitimate — the *initiation* was human.

### What This Means

| Scenario | Qualified? | Why |
|----------|:----------:|-----|
| You ask Claude to refactor | ✅ | Human-initiated, real task |
| A cron job sends queries | ❌ | Automated, scripted |

## Section 3.4 — One Account Per Person

One account. Multiple devices (server + desktop) is fine — same person, same payout.

## Recommendation

**Don't automate initiation.** The arbitrage comes from routing to **free models** ($0 cost), not from sending fake requests.
